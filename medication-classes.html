Medication Class Interaction Guides

Clinician-friendly starting points for high-yield MedCheck reviews: pharmacogenomics, CYP inhibition or induction, active metabolites, transporters, bleeding risk, QT risk, and PK exposure.

Anticoagulants and Antiplatelets

High-value because bleeding risk, CYP2C9/VKORC1 context, NSAIDs, SSRIs, azoles, antiplatelet activation, and transporter effects can all overlap.

Warfarin + fluconazole + ibuprofen Clopidogrel + omeprazole with CYP2C19 poor metabolism Apixaban + clarithromycin

Psychiatry and Neurology

Useful for CYP2D6 and CYP2C19 phenotype shifts, QT stacking, serotonin toxicity, anticholinergic burden, seizure threshold, and active-metabolite exceptions.

Paroxetine + fluoxetine washout and CYP2D6 inhibition Amitriptyline + diazepam + diphenhydramine with CYP2D6 poor metabolism Lamotrigine + valproic acid
CYP2D6CYP2C19serotonin/QTburden

Cardiology, QT Risk, and Antiarrhythmics

Prioritizes narrow-therapeutic-index exposure, QT stacking, CYP2D6 antiarrhythmic metabolism, macrolide or azole inhibition, and electrolyte-sensitive combinations.

Flecainide + fluoxetine with CYP2D6 poor metabolism Amiodarone + warfarin Sotalol + azithromycin
QTCYP2D6NTI

Antibiotics, Antifungals, and Antivirals

Often interaction-heavy because azoles, macrolides, rifamycins, boosters, and antivirals can strongly inhibit or induce CYP3A4, CYP2C9, P-gp, or OATP pathways.

Simvastatin + clarithromycin Warfarin + trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole Tacrolimus + voriconazole
CYP3A4CYP2C9P-gp

Oncology, Immunology, and Transplant

High-risk because many drugs have narrow therapeutic windows, prodrug activation, transporter relevance, genotype actionability, and severe inhibitor or inducer sensitivity.

Tacrolimus + fluconazole Azathioprine + allopurinol with TPMT poor metabolism Tamoxifen + paroxetine with CYP2D6 poor metabolism
